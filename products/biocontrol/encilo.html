Overview

EnCilo utilizes the entomopathogenic fungus Paecilomyces lilacinus, a beneficial microorganism known for its ability to infect and suppress insect populations. It offers excellent control of whiteflies, aphids, and mealybugs while remaining compatible with beneficial insects and pollinators.

Mode of Action

Spores of Paecilomyces lilacinus adhere to insect cuticles, germinate, and penetrate through the outer layers using enzymatic activity. The fungus proliferates inside the host, consuming internal tissues and causing mortality. It then sporulates on the cadaver, releasing new spores that spread to other pest populations.
