Overview

Mycova contains the entomopathogenic fungus Beauveria bassiana (1Ã—1010 CFU/g), known for its ability to naturally infect and control various insect pests. It offers an environmentally responsible alternative to synthetic insecticides, ensuring crop safety and residue-free protection.

Mode of Action

The fungal spores attach to the insect cuticle, germinate, and penetrate the exoskeleton, eventually colonizing the insect body. The fungus releases enzymes and toxins that lead to insect death, after which the fungus sporulates and spreads to other pests.
