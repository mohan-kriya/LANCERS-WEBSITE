Rexora — Metarhizium anisopliae-Based Biopesticide

Rexora is a soil-applied entomopathogenic biopesticide containing Metarhizium anisopliae spores (1×1010 CFU/g). It provides long-lasting protection against root grubs, weevils, and other soil-dwelling pests, promoting healthier root systems and sustainable pest control through natural infection and colonization.

Rexora Formulations

Rexora

Liquid formulation with high spore viability for soil and drench applications.

Download Product Sheet

Overview

Rexora utilizes the entomopathogenic fungus Metarhizium anisopliae, a naturally occurring soil microorganism that infects and kills insect pests. It targets soil-dwelling insects such as grubs and weevils while improving root zone microbial activity and plant vigor.

Mode of Action

The fungal spores adhere to the insect cuticle, germinate, and penetrate through enzymatic activity. Once inside, the fungus multiplies within the insect body, producing toxins that lead to death. It continues to proliferate, releasing spores back into the soil to infect other pests.

Key Features

  • Contains 1×1010 CFU/g viable spores for strong pest infection potential.
  • Long-lasting persistence in the rhizosphere for continuous pest suppression.
  • Safe for beneficial soil microbes and non-target organisms.
  • Reduces chemical pesticide load while enhancing soil biodiversity.
  • Suitable for organic farming and sustainable soil management programs.

Compatibility

  • Compatible with most organic soil inputs and biofertilizers.
  • Avoid mixing with fungicides or chemical insecticides.
  • Apply during evening hours for optimal spore survival.

Target Use & Benefits

Recommended Crops: Sugarcane, vegetables, fruits, plantation crops, pulses, and cereals.

Target Pests: Root grubs, weevils, termites, and other soil-dwelling insects.

Key Benefits: Natural, sustainable soil pest management promoting healthier roots and improved plant growth.
