Overview

Biota harnesses the power of Trichoderma viride and Trichoderma harzianum (1Ã—108 CFU/g), fungi known for their antagonistic properties against soil-borne pathogens such as Fusarium, Pythium, and Rhizoctonia. It improves plant root strength, nutrient uptake, and overall soil microflora balance.

Mode of Action

Trichoderma species act through mycoparasitism, competition, and antibiosis. They parasitize harmful fungi by coiling around their hyphae, releasing enzymes, and degrading cell walls. Additionally, they promote root growth by producing hormones like auxins and gibberellins.
